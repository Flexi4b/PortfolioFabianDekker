Skeletons & Sorcery

Skeletons & Sorcery is a rouge-like card game.

You have entered this tower to climb to the top, but all the creatures in this tower are trying to kick you out.


This game is playable on Itch.io


Made by:

Artists: Luna Oppatja, Santos Geerman, Vic Ramjiawansingh, Lee Lotus Rijbeek, Louisa Burfitt.

Developers: Elgin Huisden, Casper Ketelaars & Fabian Dekker.

My Contributions:

Warrior Enemy

Whenever the player ends their turn, it is time for the enemies to attack. The warrior attacks the player every turn, that is, until the warrior gets to half of his full health and gives itself an attack buff. When the warrior has the attack buff active, it will deal double damage to the player. This buff will only trigger once, this is so the warrior won't get too strong when it gets healed by the healer enemy.

For this to work, I made 3 states for the warrior to be in. This way, I can make the warrior do his actions whenever I choose. His states are: Attacking, buff themselves and waiting. When the attacking state is active, it will attack the player. Buff themselves occurs when the warrior's health reaches half. State waiting is used before or after the warrior's turn, this is so the player & other enemies have a chance to do their actions.

Enemies Animations

All enemies have animations for all their actions. They all have an: Start, Idle, Attack, Buff/Heal/Taunt, Hit and Death animation. I needed to make sure that all the animations got triggered to play and all at the right time. The warrior enemy was the most difficult to figure out when to play its animation and to not mess with the other code that was already written. But after making all the animations work, it was much easier to code the animation triggers for the healer and tank enemies.

Learn more about my code here


Room Switch

After defeating the enemies in 2 separate rooms will the layout change. This is done so that the player is not playing the whole game in the same environment and to show all the props that have been made by the artist. There are 5 rooms in total, and after all five rooms have been shown, the next room will be random. To make sure, the randomization didn't choose the same room multiple times in a row, I put in a check for if the next randomized room is the same as the room that was active before. If these rooms are the same, the randomizer will pick a new room and starts the process again, checking if it is the same room as before.
